# Case Study: Stakeholder Engagement via Power–Interest Matrix

## 🎯 Purpose
Improve stakeholder satisfaction and communication by mapping influence (power) against interest in project outcomes.  
This framework helped ensure the right level of visibility and engagement for each stakeholder group.

---

## 📝 Context
- **Project:** PCMS – Configuring parameters and managing real‑time operational data of wind turbines.  
- **Role:** Senior Scrum Master | Facilitator of stakeholder alignment.  
- **Challenge:** Stakeholders had varying levels of interest and power, leading to misaligned expectations and communication gaps.

---

## 🚀 Actions Taken
1. **Mapped Stakeholders**
   - Identified stakeholders by **Power (decision‑making authority)** and **Interest (engagement in project outcomes)**.  
   - Example:  
     - **Global Head of Department** → High power, low interest.  
     - **Key Product Stakeholder** → Medium power, high interest.  
     - **Engineering Team Lead** → Medium power, high interest.  
     - **Peer Team Member** → Low power, low interest.

2. **Tailored Communication**
   - **High Power / Low Interest** → Concise executive dashboards, only critical updates.  
   - **High Power / High Interest** → Detailed sprint reviews, PI planning visibility.  
   - **Low Power / High Interest** → Regular updates, inclusion in review calls.  
   - **Low Power / Low Interest** → Minimal updates, optional involvement.

3. **Applied Beyond Work**
   - Used the same matrix creatively in personal contexts (e.g., family event planning) to balance influence vs. interest.

---

## 📊 Outcomes
- Stakeholder Happiness Ratings improved from **2/5 → 4.5/5**.  
- Reduced miscommunication by aligning reporting style to stakeholder needs.  
- Increased trust and transparency across distributed teams.

---

## 🌟 Key Takeaway
The **Power–Interest Matrix** is a simple yet powerful tool for Scrum Masters to **prioritize communication, manage expectations, and elevate stakeholder satisfaction**.
