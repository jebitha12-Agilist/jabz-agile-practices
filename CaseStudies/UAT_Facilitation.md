# Case Study: Facilitating UAT for Agile Delivery

## 🎯 Context
Project: PCMS – Platform for configuring parameters and managing real-time operational data of wind turbines.  
Role: Senior Scrum Master | Impediment remover, mentor, coach, facilitator.  
Tools: Jira, Kendis, Confluence, REST APIs, MongoDB.

---

## 📝 Challenge
- Stakeholders lacked clarity on **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)** scope.  
- Dependencies across distributed teams created delays.  
- Feedback loops were slow, impacting release confidence.

---

## 🚀 Actions Taken
1. **Prepared Stakeholders**
   - Conducted UAT readiness workshops.  
   - Shared acceptance criteria and scope via Confluence dashboards.  

2. **Facilitated UAT Execution**
   - Created a Jira workflow: *To Do → In Progress → Ready for UAT → Done*.  
   - Ensured blockers were resolved within 24 hours.  

3. **Enhanced Transparency**
   - Designed executive dashboards showing UAT progress and defect trends.  
   - Used Kendis PI boards to synchronize dependencies across ARTs.  

4. **Continuous Improvement**
   - Captured UAT feedback as backlog items.  
   - Facilitated retrospectives with stakeholders to refine the process.

---

## 📊 Outcomes
- Reduced UAT cycle time by **20%**.  
- Increased stakeholder confidence scores by **40%**.  
- Achieved **95% story completion rate** with improved predictability.  

---

## 🌟 Key Takeaway
Effective UAT facilitation requires **clear scope, transparent dashboards, and fast feedback loops**. As a Scrum Master, I enabled stakeholders to validate business value confidently before release.
