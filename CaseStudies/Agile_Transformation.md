# Case Study: Agile Transformation of Legacy Teams

## 🎯 Context
Project: WDM – Migration of legacy on‑premise databases to cloud architecture.  
Role: Senior Scrum Master | SAFe Team‑level coach.  
Tools: Azure Boards, Kendis, SAFe 6.0, Databricks.

---

## 📝 Challenge
- Teams were accustomed to **traditional waterfall workflows**.  
- Resistance to change slowed adoption of Agile practices.  
- Stakeholder confidence was low due to unpredictable delivery timelines.

---

## 🚀 Actions Taken
1. **Agile Workshops**
   - Designed and facilitated customized workshops to introduce Scrum values.  
   - Coached teams on ceremonies, backlog refinement, and self‑organization.

2. **SAFe Implementation**
   - Integrated teams into the **Agile Release Train (ART)**.  
   - Facilitated PI Planning and ART Syncs to align dependencies across programs.

3. **Backlog Restructuring**
   - Partnered with Product Owners to remediate disordered backlogs.  
   - Introduced **Definition of Ready (DoR)** and **Definition of Done (DoD)** criteria.

4. **Transparency & Metrics**
   - Established dashboards with burn‑down charts, velocity tracking, and WIP limits.  
   - Shared delivery health metrics with stakeholders to build trust.

---

## 📊 Outcomes
- Transitioned a legacy team into **Scrum framework** within 6 months.  
- Boosted Sprint Commitment Reliability by **30%**.  
- Raised stakeholder happiness ratings significantly through transparent reporting.  
- Increased delivery confidence across distributed teams.

---

## 🌟 Key Takeaway
Agile transformation requires **coaching, structured ceremonies, and transparent metrics**. By enabling self‑organization and aligning stakeholders, legacy teams can evolve into high‑performing Agile units.
