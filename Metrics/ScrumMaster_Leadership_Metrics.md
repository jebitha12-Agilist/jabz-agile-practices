# Scrum Master – Leadership Call Metrics 📊

## 🎯 Purpose
Provide leadership with clear, quantifiable insights into **team performance, stakeholder handling, and enterprise alignment** during Agile delivery.

---

## 🌀 Sprint Execution
- **BUR (Burn‑up Progress)** → Scope completed vs. committed, with scope change markers.  
- **VEL (Velocity Trend)** → Average story points delivered per sprint, with variance.  
- **CR (Commitment Reliability)** → % of committed work completed (predictability metric).  

---

## 🔍 Quality & Flow
- **DEF (Defect Leakage)** → Bugs found post‑sprint vs. within sprint.  
- **CT (Cycle Time)** → Average time from “In Progress” to “Done.”  
- **BLK (Blocked Items)** → Count and resolution time for impediments.  

---

## 🤝 Stakeholder Handling
- **VIS (Visibility Index)** → % of stakeholders reviewing sprint burn‑up charts.  
- **ENG (Engagement Level)** → Attendance rate in sprint reviews and PI demos.  
- **FIR (Feedback Incorporation Rate)** → % of stakeholder feedback addressed within the next sprint.  
- **ESC (Escalation Rate)** → Number of stakeholder escalations per sprint.  

---

## 🌐 Cross‑Team & Enterprise
- **DEP (Dependency Resolution Time)** → Average time to unblock cross‑team dependencies.  
- **ALN (Alignment Score)** → % of scope changes communicated and accepted by stakeholders.  
- **RR (Release Readiness)** → % of features meeting Release DoD criteria.  

---

## 📣 Leadership Call Framing
- **Visuals** → Burn‑up chart with scope change markers.  
- **Narrative** → “This sprint delivered X% of committed scope, with Y% scope added mid‑sprint. Stakeholder feedback was incorporated within Z days.”  
- **Impact** → Tie metrics to business outcomes (predictability, compliance, stakeholder trust).  

---

## 🌟 Key Takeaway
Scrum Master metrics for leadership should highlight **predictability, stakeholder confidence, and enterprise alignment**, not just team velocity.
