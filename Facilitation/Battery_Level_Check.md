# Retrospective: The Battery Level Check 🔋

## 🎯 Purpose
Treat human energy as a finite team resource. Reflect not only on what we did, but how it affected our fuel levels.

---

## 🕒 60-Minute Facilitation Agenda
1. **The Hook (0–5m)** → GIF‑o‑Meter icebreaker to gauge current "charge."  
2. **Silent Writing (5–15m)** → Team adds notes to four zones privately.  
3. **The Scan (15–25m)** → Group similar notes into themes.  
4. **Dot Voting (25–30m)** → Vote on most critical drain or supercharger.  
5. **Action Workshop (30–50m)** → Apply the Three W’s (What, Who, When).  
6. **Closing (50–60m)** → Round of kudos for battery‑chargers.

---

## ⚡ Four Quadrants
- **FULL CHARGE** → What went perfectly? What energized us?  
- **POWER SAVER** → Exhausting tasks with low value.  
- **DRAINED** → Momentum killers, P0 problems to solve.  
- **SUPERCHARGER** → One change tomorrow that boosts energy.

---

## 🧩 Grouping Phase Tips
- **Silent Sort** → 3 minutes for team to cluster notes.  
- **Theme Headers** → Label clusters clearly (e.g., “Process Friction”).  
- **Duplicate Delete** → Stack identical notes with counts (x5).  
- **Parking Lot** → Move personal notes for 1‑on‑1 follow‑up.

---

## 🎭 High-Voltage Kick-off
- **GIF‑o‑Meter** → Team posts a GIF representing current battery level.  
- **Logic** → Humor builds safety; easier to show burnout via GIF than admit it directly.  
- **High‑Energy GIFs** → Focus on ambitious superchargers.  
- **Low‑Energy GIFs** → Focus on fixing
